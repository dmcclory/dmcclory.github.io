We're going to fill this thing up with an awful lot of content. The ghoal is to have a bunch of paragraphs of text, each of which can and ought to be arbitrarily long. The idea is to see what multi-line paragraphs look like, in order to get a an appropriate line spacing and line width. And once we've done that, we can focus on other things.

Like making the page layout sing.

And so let's just spitball. Let's say they hold the tryouts for PHIT house teams twice a year. The last one was in October, which was 3 months ago. So maybe they'll hold auditions again in April to June? By that time I'll have finished 301. Even if they hold them in March, I'll have finished 301. As far as I can tell, there are a number of people on teams who haven't been through all of the classes - Max, Mallory X, Scott Campbell. So having been through the classes isn't necessary condition for getting on a team. What is a necessary condition for getting on a team? that's a good question. I'll add it to the list of open questions. There was another thread, to visit. Ok, so if they hold them in April, I could definitely be in good shape. And if I don't make it, first of all, I won't die, but second of all, there'll be other opportunities and other teams to join, and probably another set of auditions at the end of the year.

Wierd thought: what is the rate at which teams die? When they add three teams, that'll bring the total up to 10. 2 are kinda newer. And now that I think of it, some of those other teams are only like a year or a year and a half old. And they didn't exist a few years ago. Maybe teams only last like 2 or 3 years? 3 years is a long time to be doing something. So some of the teams won't exist next year or in two years. Who will go first? Sabotage? Doctor Sleepover? Big Baby?

A whole 'nother angle is: writing scripts/sketches. I'll turn my attention to this later, but for now I think I have enough content to try out layouts with real text. Thank you brain! for having an endless amount of stuff to churn and type out!
